# Stakeholders List

### Stakeholders for the Child Safety Dashcam System
* **Dashcam Owners**: Individuals who purchase and install the dashcam system in their vehicles, interested in the system's ease of use and effectiveness in locating missing children.
* **Parents of Missing Children**: Families of missing children who would benefit from the system's ability to quickly disseminate information and potentially locate their loved ones.
* **Children**: Minors who may be reported missing and whose images and information would be used in the system, with a concern for their safety and privacy.
* **Pedestrians**: People who walk or engage in other activities near areas where dashcams are installed, potentially being captured on camera and having their privacy impacted.
* **Law Enforcement Agencies**: Organizations responsible for investigating missing person cases, interested in the system's ability to provide valuable leads and assistance in their work.
* **Non-profit Organizations Focused on Child Safety**: Groups dedicated to advocating for and supporting the safety and well-being of children, concerned with the system's potential to aid in their mission.
* **Privacy Advocates**: Individuals and groups who champion the right to privacy, potentially concerned about the collection, storage, and use of personal data in the system.
* **Regulatory Bodies**: Government agencies responsible for overseeing data protection, surveillance, and consumer rights, ensuring the system complies with relevant laws and regulations.
* **Competitors in the Dashcam Market**: Companies that produce and sell dashcams without the child safety feature, interested in how this feature might impact their market share.
* **Partners in Technology and AI**: Businesses that collaborate with the dashcam system developers on AI person recognition models, interested in the system's success and potential for future collaborations.
* **Telecommunications Providers**: Companies that offer Wi-Fi and Bluetooth services used by the dashcams to communicate, interested in the system's demand for their services.
* **Vehicle Manufacturers**: Companies that produce vehicles in which the dashcams are installed, potentially interested in integrating the child safety feature into their vehicles' standard or optional equipment.
* **Insurance Companies**: Providers of vehicle insurance, potentially interested in offering discounts to dashcam owners who install the child safety system, thereby reducing the risk of accidents or liabilities.
* **School Administrations**: Educational institutions concerned with the safety of their students, potentially interested in integrating the child safety system into their overall safety protocols.
* **Community Leaders**: Local government officials and leaders of community groups, interested in the system's potential to enhance public safety and reduce crime in their areas.
* **Data Security Experts**: Professionals responsible for ensuring the secure storage and transmission of sensitive data, concerned with protecting the system from cyber threats and data breaches.
